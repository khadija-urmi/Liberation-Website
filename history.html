History of the Liberation War

Causes of the War

  • Political discrimination: East Pakistan won the 1970 election, but power was not transferred to Sheikh Mujibur Rahman and the elected representatives.
  • Economic inequality: East Pakistan generated major revenue but received less development support than West Pakistan.
  • Cultural and linguistic suppression: Bengali identity, language, and culture were repeatedly marginalized.
  • Military crackdown: On 25 March 1971, Pakistan Army launched Operation Searchlight, causing mass killings and repression.

Key Events

  • 7 March 1971: Sheikh Mujibur Rahman gave his historic speech calling for resistance.
  • 25 March 1971: Operation Searchlight began in Dhaka and other areas.
  • 26 March 1971: Declaration of Bangladesh's independence was announced.
  • April 1971: Provisional Government of Bangladesh was formed at Mujibnagar.
  • 16 December 1971: Pakistani forces surrendered; Bangladesh achieved victory.
